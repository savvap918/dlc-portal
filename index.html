Sales Playbook

Always Be Closing. Get That Lead.

01
Overview
What we do, how it works, and why it's an easy sell
What we do
We run automated B2B lead generation for house cleaning companies. We contact 150–200 local real estate agents per city on the client's behalf, qualify replies with AI, and forward warm leads directly to the cleaning company. They pay 15% commission only when a job completes — no retainer, no setup fee.
Your Positioning
"I run an automated system that gets your cleaning company booked for move-out jobs by contacting real estate agents in your city — and you only pay me 15% when a job actually completes."
→You're not selling a service. You're offering a risk-free way to grow their business. Every call you make is doing them a favour.
→Never mention AI. They don't care. Keep it simple — lead with the outcome.
→Listen more than you talk.
The Numbers

Commission rate

15%

Per completed job

Agents contacted

150–200

Per city, first month

Your cut

40%

Of what client pays

With 20–25 clients at 40% commission share: $2,000–$2,500 USD (~$2,700–$3,400 CDN) every month with zero ongoing effort after they sign.
How the System Works
  1. Cleaning company signs on → system automatically finds 150–200 local real estate agents in their city
  2. Personalized cold email sent to each agent on the client's behalf over a 3-email sequence across 7 days — zero manual work
  3. Every reply is analyzed by AI. Warm leads are instantly forwarded to the cleaning company with full agent contact details
  4. Cleaning company reports completed jobs through a dashboard → 15% commission invoiced automatically on the 1st of the following month
02
The Call
Use this as an outline, not a script. Adapt to the conversation
0:00–0:30
Step 1 — The Opener
Get past the first 15 seconds
▼
Script
"Hi, is this [Owner Name]? Great — my name is [Your Name], I was just wondering if you work with any real estate agents." "Would you be interested in building more relationships with realtors?" "I work with cleaning companies across the US and Canada to get them booked for move-out cleans through real estate agents — on a commission-only basis." "I'm not selling software or a subscription. You only pay me if I book you a job. Is that worth 90 seconds of your time?"
→"Commission-only" and "you only pay if I book you a job" are your two most powerful openers. Use them in the first 20 seconds every time.
→Asking a broad question off the bat like "do you work with any agents at the moment?" usually hooks them in quickly.
→Use this as an outline. Not as a script. Adapt the opener to whatever works for you.
0:30–2:00
Step 2 — Discovery
Understand their business before pitching anything
▼

Ask 2–3 of these. Have a conversation, not an interrogation:

  • "Are move-out cleans a big part of your business right now, or more occasional?"
  • "Do you already have relationships with any real estate agents who send you work?"
  • "What's your biggest challenge with getting consistent move-out bookings?"
  • "If I could get you 3–5 new realtor relationships in [City] this month, would you have capacity for more jobs?"
→Already have realtor relationships? → Position this as adding more.
→Zero realtor relationships? → You're solving an urgent pain. Lean in.
→No capacity right now? → Park it. "Want me to reach back out in 30 days?"
2:00–4:00
Step 3 — The Pitch
Plain English, no jargon
▼
Script
"Here's what I do. I run an automated outreach system that contacts real estate agents in [City] on your behalf." "It sends them professional emails introducing your business as a move-out clean specialist. When an agent replies and says they're interested, I forward you their contact details immediately." "You follow up, quote the job, and if it completes — you pay me 15% of whatever you invoiced." "There's no monthly fee, no setup cost, no contract. If I don't get you a job, you pay nothing." "In a typical city, we contact 150–200 agents over the first month. Even if only 5% convert, that's 7–10 new realtor relationships for your business."
4:00–5:00
Step 4 — The Value Stack
Make the math obvious
▼
Script
"Think about what one realtor relationship is worth to you." "A busy agent might refer you 2–3 move-out cleans a month. At even $300 a job, that's $600–$900 a month from a single contact." "My 15% on that is $90–$135. You're keeping $510–$775 from business you didn't have before." "And I'm not going after one agent — I'm contacting hundreds of them on your behalf, automatically, while you focus on doing the work."
→Let them do the math themselves. Silence after "business you didn't have before" is your friend. Don't fill it.
5:00–6:00
Step 5 — The Close
Get a decision or a next step
▼
Script
"All I need from you to get started is your business name, the city you serve, and an email address." "I set everything up on my end — you don't have to do anything until a warm lead lands in your inbox." "Does that sound like something worth trying?"
→Try to close on the first call. If they're not enthusiastic, at minimum get a meeting booked before you hang up.
→All you need: business name, city, email. Keep the ask tiny.

Call or text

→This forces a real answer. "Reach out later" keeps the door open. "Not a fit" lets you move on. Both beat silence.

04
Ground Rules
These apply to every rep on every call
  1. 1

    Lead with the outcome, never the process. "We get realtors calling your business" beats explaining the tech. One sentence max if they ask how it works.

  2. 2

    Get a micro-commitment before hanging up. Before ending the first call: "Can I get your email so I can send over a quick recap?" A small yes makes the next contact feel natural.

  3. 3

    Never say "just following up." Always have a reason — a result, a city availability hook, a question. If you have no reason, use the follow-up scripts.

  4. 4

    Log every contact in the tracker the same day. Date, channel, what they said, next action. If it's not in the sheet, it doesn't exist.

  5. 5

    One cleaning company per city. Use this as a genuine urgency hook — "we only work with one company per city" is a real reason to move fast. Don't give it away lightly.

  6. 6

    Don't pitch over objections. If someone pushes back, acknowledge it: "Totally fair — what's the main thing you're unsure about?" Pushing kills deals. Space and respect close them.

  7. 7

    Try to close on the first call. If they're not biting, at minimum get a meeting booked before you hang up. Never leave a call with no next step.

05
Objections
How to handle the most common pushbacks

"I need to think about it."

Totally — what's the main thing you're unsure about? Usually there's one specific concern under this. Get it out in the open. If they genuinely need time, lock in a specific callback: "Want me to call you Thursday?"

"How do I know this actually works?"

Fair question. [Use any real result you have.] If you don't have one yet: "We only make money when you do — so we're only signing companies we're confident we can deliver for. You're not risking anything."

"I already have enough work."

Great — that means you have capacity to grow. Most of our clients use it to fill gaps in slow seasons or justify hiring their next cleaner. No pressure to start now — want me to check back in a couple months?

"What's the catch?"

Honestly, there isn't one from your side. You only pay 15% when you complete a job from one of our referrals. If nothing comes through, you owe us nothing. The only thing we ask is that you report completed jobs back to us so we can invoice correctly.

"We do our own marketing."

Completely understand — this isn't replacing that. Think of it as one extra channel running in the background. You're not paying for it unless it produces, so it doesn't compete with anything you're already doing.

"15% sounds high."

Think about what you'd pay to acquire a customer any other way — Google ads, flyers, referral fees. This is 15% only on jobs that actually complete. If a job doesn't happen, it costs you nothing. You're paying for a result, not a promise.
